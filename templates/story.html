Hello, and thank you for taking the time to review my portfolio!

Originally from Syracuse, NY, I worked for Syrasoft, a software company providing financial and management software solutions to self-storage companies across the country. I acted as a Software Support Analyst and Large Account Manager gaining a firm understanding of software development workflows and translating client’s business needs into deliverable software. Daily responsibilities included troubleshooting client issues, providing data repairs using SQL, coding custom installers for large accounts specific business rules, training users of the software, and migrating clients from existing software to our own.

  In 2015 I moved to North Carolina’s Research Triangle to work for Farragut Systems, a leading producer of local government software solutions. Although my title stayed the same, my expertise and abilities grew exponentially. In addition to my existing skills I was responsible for coding complex stored procedures, performing routine maintenance and troubleshooting on a large SQL Server Databases, troubleshooting issues for clients using our cloud-based SaaS, leading large conferences, managing documentation and writing complex guides for business processes, and more.

  My experience leading conferences inspired me to seek a new challenge. I had a desire to influence my community and work as a High School Educator, introducing students to one of my passions, History. Teaching became my most challenging accomplishment! Convincing teenagers the value of education and understanding the countries history proved far differnt from educating paid employees on more efficent business practices. Countless hours spent up late lesson planning , providing meaningful feedback, and strategizing managment eventually paid off. Students achieved fantastic results on state assessments, I was awarded with the "going green" award for 2 years, was recognized as beginner teacher of the month twice, and now act as the Team Lead of the American History department.

  I wish to transition back to the software industry. My wife and I brought our first child into the world, and with that change I decided to focus my life on a passion that allows me to spend quality time with my family. Although teaching is a fantastic profession, it requires a huge amount of mental energy to excel. I know that I could be a decent teacher, and still have the energy at the end of the day for my family, but I am not one that is ok being "decent" at anything. I strive for excellence in my life.I have spent the better part of a year teaching myself to program. I have used languages ranging from C, C#, Visual Basic, JavaScript, Python, Flask, HTML, and CSS. As I seek to return to the software industry, it is withnew knowledge and experience coding in a variety of languages. I possess the skills to be a great educator, empathetic professional support, an experienced troubleshooter, and possess an understanding of development languages and practices.

  I hope to work with you all one day in the future, and if this website and my story appeal to you, let me know! I love learning and sharing life experiences with new people!
